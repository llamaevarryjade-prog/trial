Places to Visit

Sapat Dam

The Sapat Dam, officially known as the Sulvec Small Reservoir Irrigation Project (SRIP), is a modern and vital piece of infrastructure located in Barangay Sapat, Pasuquin, in the province of Ilocos Norte. Inaugurated in July 2024, the dam is a significant achievement for the region, addressing longstanding challenges related to agricultural water supply. It represents not only a feat of engineering but also a beacon of hope for the local farming communities that have long depended on erratic water sources for their livelihoods.

Maang-angri Cave

Deep within the rugged, mountainous terrain of Barangay Sapat in Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte, lies the alluring and challenging Maang-angri Cave. Roughly translating to "stinky cave," its name is believed to come from the scent of guano left by its extensive population of fruit bats, a common characteristic of many caves. Yet, this name does little to capture the true magnificence that awaits those brave enough to venture inside. This vast limestone cave is widely regarded as one of the best caving sites in the province, a paradise for experienced spelunkers, and a treasure trove for nature enthusiasts.

King Kong Paradise

Aptly nicknamed King Kong Paradise, this breathtaking natural wonder in Barangay Sulbec, Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte, is a thrilling haven for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Named for its towering rock formations that evoke the rugged and mythical landscape of the movie King Kong, this cascading waterfall offers an unforgettable outdoor experience deep within the province's lush wilderness.

Sexy Beach

Nestled in Barangay Estancia, Sexy Beach in Pasuquin, Ilocos Norte, is a stunning stretch of coastline known for its breathtaking beauty and tranquil ambiance. Unlike more crowded destinations, this beach offers a serene escape, allowing visitors to unwind and savor the majestic seascape in relative peace.
